# Personas

Fill each section with real interview quotes, purchase triggers, and objections.
Update as you learn from support tickets and sales calls.

## Couples planning a wedding (primary)

- Who: engaged couples 6–12 months pre-wedding, US/EU, planning together
- Job to be done: capture guest memories they can revisit forever
- Trigger: sending save-the-dates, choosing wedding website
- Objections: "Do guests actually use QR codes?", "Is it another app to download?"
- Buys when: sample book convinces them the output is emotionally worth it
- Search behavior: mixed brand + generic ("wedding guest book alternatives", "QR guest book")

## Wedding planners (high-LTV)

- Who: independent planners + boutique agencies, 20–100 events/yr
- Job to be done: differentiate client experience without adding vendor overhead
- Trigger: client asks "how do we make our guest book memorable?"
- Objections: pricing structure, white-label question, learning curve
- Buys when: they see it works for a peer planner
- Search behavior: professional-intent ("guest book for wedding planners", "modern alternative to guest book")

## Parents & family gifters (secondary)

- Who: parents of the couple, close family, wedding party
- Job to be done: give something meaningful, not another kitchen gadget
- Trigger: registry looks impersonal, wanting to contribute to the day itself
- Objections: "Will the couple actually use it?"
- Buys when: they see how it turns into a keepsake

## Guests (funnel-adjacent, not payer)

- Who: attends the wedding, uses the product briefly
- Job to be done: leave a message quickly without friction
- Trigger: scans QR at ceremony/reception
- Not a paying persona, but conversion signal — every guest is a future couple
